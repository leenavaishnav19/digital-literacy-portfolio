A common example of poor digital communication causing problems is when a student posts inappropriate or offensive content on social media without considering its impact. For instance, there have been cases where students lost internship opportunities because recruiters found unprofessional posts on their profiles. Such actions can damage one’s reputation and create a negative impression.

This situation could have been avoided if the student had followed responsible social media practices, such as thinking before posting, maintaining professionalism, and using privacy settings wisely. Additionally, being aware that digital content is permanent and can be viewed by anyone, including employers, is very important. Practicing respectful communication and sharing meaningful content can help build a positive digital identity and prevent such issues in the future.
