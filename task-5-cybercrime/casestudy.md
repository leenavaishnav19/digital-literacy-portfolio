# 🔐 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It involves tricking users into authorizing fraudulent transactions through apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

A typical scam begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. The fraudster creates urgency and asks the victim to click on a malicious link or approve a “request” in their UPI app. In many cases, the victim is told that they will receive money, but instead they unknowingly approve a payment request. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

College students and new digital users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI requests work. People who frequently use online payments or sell items online are also common targets.

The consequences can include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the stolen money. Victims may also lose trust in digital platforms.

This type of fraud can be prevented by understanding that entering a UPI PIN always means sending money, never receiving it. Awareness and caution are key to avoiding such scams.
